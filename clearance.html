Clearance Level System

The SCP Foundation employs a hierarchical security clearance system to regulate access to sensitive information and restricted areas. Personnel must strictly adhere to clearance level restrictions. Unauthorized access attempts will result in immediate termination.

Level 0

Uncleared Personnel

Access Rights
  • Public areas only
  • Non-classified information
  • Basic facility amenities
Eligible Personnel
  • Maintenance staff
  • Janitorial crews
  • Contract workers
Restrictions
  • No access to SCP information
  • No access to secure areas
  • Constant supervision required
Level 1

Confidential

Access Rights
  • Basic containment areas
  • Low-risk SCP files
  • Standard security protocols
Eligible Personnel
  • Junior researchers
  • Security officers
  • Medical staff
Restrictions
  • No access to Euclid/Keter containment
  • Limited research participation
  • Supervised access only
Level 2

Restricted

Access Rights
  • Most containment areas
  • Research laboratories
  • Security systems
Eligible Personnel
  • Senior researchers
  • Security supervisors
  • Containment specialists
Restrictions
  • Limited Keter access
  • No O5 documentation
  • Restricted experiment participation
Level 3

Secret

Access Rights
  • All containment zones
  • Keter-class research
  • Emergency protocols
Eligible Personnel
  • Site directors
  • Project heads
  • MTF commanders
Restrictions
  • Limited O5 access
  • Restricted facility locations
  • Classified project limitations
Level 4

Top Secret

Access Rights
  • All Foundation facilities
  • Classified research
  • O5 documentation
Eligible Personnel
  • Regional directors
  • Ethics Committee
  • Department heads
Restrictions
  • Limited Thaumiel access
  • O5 approval required
  • Overseer restrictions apply

SECURITY NOTICE

Attempting to access information or areas above your clearance level is a severe breach of security protocols. Any violation will result in immediate termination and administration of Class-A amnestics. All access attempts are logged and monitored.
