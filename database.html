Object Classifications

Safe

Anomalies that are easily contained and pose minimal risk. Standard containment procedures are sufficient.

Euclid

Anomalies that require more resources to contain and/or are not fully understood. Containment procedures may be complex.

Keter

Anomalies that are exceedingly difficult to contain or pose a significant threat. Special containment procedures required.

Thaumiel

Anomalies used to contain other SCPs. Information restricted to high-level personnel only.

Apollyon

Anomalies that cannot be contained and pose an existential threat. World-ending implications.

Neutralized

Anomalies that have been permanently disabled, destroyed, or otherwise rendered inert.

ACCESS RESTRICTIONS

Unauthorized access to classified files will result in immediate termination and administration of Class A amnestics. All database queries are logged and monitored. Some files may require additional clearance levels or O5 Council approval.
